# Multiplayer Modes

## Design intent

Multiplayer should support classic RTS skirmish and objective-driven modes that make the world identity matter.

## Core modes

### 1v1 Duel
Standard competitive format.

Recommended map size: small/medium.

Win conditions:
- destroy main command structure;
- surrender;
- optional objective victory disabled by default.

### 2v2 Alliance War
Team mode with combined faction synergies.

Design focus:
- shared fronts;
- resource specialization;
- hero combinations;
- map objectives.

### 3-Player War of Crowns
Free-for-all or locked triangle: Ferrum vs Aurelia vs Thalassia.

Design focus:
- diplomacy pressure;
- kingmaker risk;
- neutral objectives;
- trade and betrayal.

### Co-op Ashlands Crisis
Two or more players defend against escalating Ashlands corruption.

Design focus:
- PvE waves;
- boss phases;
- shared containment;
- relic objectives.

### Naval Supremacy
Thalassia-style map mode focused on ports, convoys and sea lanes.

Design focus:
- water maps;
- port capture;
- convoy escort;
- mixed amphibious assaults.

### Dominion Siege
Asymmetric mode: one player defends fortified Ferrum position, another attacks with superior numbers or timed objectives.

## Ranked recommendation

Initial ranked modes:

- 1v1 Duel;
- 2v2 Alliance War.

Experimental modes should remain unranked until balance matures.

## Match duration targets

| Mode | Target duration |
|---|---:|
| 1v1 | 18-32 minutes |
| 2v2 | 25-40 minutes |
| FFA | 35-60 minutes |
| Co-op Crisis | 30-45 minutes |
| Naval Supremacy | 25-45 minutes |

## Anti-stalemate systems

- escalating neutral objectives;
- limited high-tier resource nodes;
- Ashlands corruption events after long games;
- trade lane decay under contest;
- super-tech visibility warnings.
