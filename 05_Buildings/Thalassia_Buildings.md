# Thalassia Buildings

## Tide Hall

### Role
Main command building, worker production, Tide Favor generation.

### Gameplay
Tide Hall is weaker than Iron Hall but more flexible. It should support expansion through ports, markets and water control.

### Upgrades
- Harbor Compact — improves nearby coastal structures.
- Council Charter — increases Trade Coin generation.
- Tide Registry — unlocks advanced naval production.

## Reef Barracks

### Role
Primary land unit production.

### Trains
- Coral Guard;
- Tide Spearman;
- Mist Skirmisher;
- Pearlguard after tech.

## Coral Market

### Role
Economic and trade structure.

### Gameplay
Generates Coin when connected to active trade lanes or ports.

### Upgrades
- Salt Contracts;
- Coral Tools;
- Merchant Ledgers.

## Whitewood Dock

### Role
Naval production.

### Produces
- Whitewood Cutter;
- Pearl Barge;
- Tide Galleon;
- Grand Galleon after tier upgrade.

## Weaver Basin

### Role
Caster production and Tide Favor upgrades.

### Trains
- Water Weaver;
- Stormcaller Weaver.

### Research
- Mist Nets;
- Current Sails;
- Tidal Shielding.

## Mist Beacon

### Role
Defensive and stealth support.

### Gameplay
Creates localized mist fields. Linked Mist Beacons create a network that improves Thalassian movement and vision denial.

## Grand Dock

### Role
Tier 3 naval structure.

### Produces
- Grand Galleon;
- Tidal Siege Platform;
- Command Barge.

## Pearl Archive

### Role
Advanced research and diplomacy structure.

### Unlocks
- Crown of the Sea Roads;
- Deep Current Maps;
- Port Capture Doctrine;
- Saltwater Ward.
