# Thalassia Core Units

## Worker: Tidewright

### Role
Builder, dock worker, route maintainer.

### Abilities
- Build coastal and standard structures;
- Repair ships;
- Mark Trade Buoy on water routes.

## Infantry: Coral Guard

### Role
Flexible frontline infantry.

### Strengths
- stronger near water;
- moderate cost;
- good mixed-army unit.

### Weaknesses
- loses efficiency deep inland;
- weaker than heavy Ferrum infantry.

## Spear: Tide Spearman

### Role
Anti-cavalry, anti-monster near water.

### Ability
**Wet Ground Brace** — creates small slow field if fighting near water.

## Ranged: Mist Skirmisher

### Role
Harassment and stealth ranged unit.

### Ability
**Mist Cloak** — gains camouflage inside mist fields or near Mist Beacons.

## Caster: Water Weaver

### Role
Terrain control, displacement, shields.

### Abilities
- Current Pull;
- Mist Veil;
- Tidal Shield.

## Support: Reef Engineer

### Role
Builds buoys, repairs ships, deploys floating barriers.

### Abilities
- Deploy Reef Barrier;
- Patch Hull;
- Improve Trade Lane.

## Naval Light: Whitewood Cutter

### Role
Fast scout and raider.

### Strengths
- fast;
- good against trade ships;
- can reveal coastal fog.

## Naval Heavy: Tide Galleon

### Role
Primary naval warship.

### Strengths
- high durability for Thalassia;
- broadside attack;
- benefits from Tide Favor.

## Transport: Pearl Barge

### Role
Unit transport and supply ship.

### Weakness
Requires escort.

## Elite: Pearlguard

### Role
Elite defensive infantry for ports and palace maps.

### Ability
**Pearl Oath** — receives shield when defending controlled ports.
