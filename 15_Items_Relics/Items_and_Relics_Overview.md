# Items and Relics Overview

## Purpose

Items and relics give heroes progression, map objectives meaning, and campaign choices long-term consequences.

## Item categories

### Common items
Small stat boosts and utility effects.

Examples:
- Iron Ration Kit — minor HP regeneration.
- Sunstone Charm — minor mana regeneration.
- Salted Bandage — reduces slow duration.
- Miner Lantern — small vision bonus.

### Uncommon items
More specialized, faction-flavored effects.

Examples:
- Forge Bracer — armor and repair bonus.
- Dawn Lens — improves spell range.
- Coral Compass — improves movement near water.
- Ash Filter Mask — reduces environmental damage.

### Rare items
Hero-defining situational tools.

Examples:
- Magnetized Signet — improves pull/control effects.
- Twin Sun Seal — increases purification power.
- Pearl Tide Map — reveals sea routes periodically.
- Obsidian Ward — reduces burst damage but slows wearer.

### Relics
Relics are major strategic rewards, often tied to map bosses or campaign missions.

Examples:
- Shard of the Star-Forge;
- Crown Fragment of Helios;
- Anchor Chain of Dreadfast;
- Tide Pearl of Aethelgard;
- Sealed Abyssal Core.

## Relic rules

- Relics should be visible on the map or tied to objectives.
- Relics should alter strategy, not only add stats.
- Relics may unlock tech, hero abilities or campaign branches.
- Abyssal relics must include risk.

## Inventory model

Recommended hero inventory:

- 4 active/passive item slots;
- 1 relic slot;
- consumables optional;
- faction-restricted relics allowed in campaign.

## Campaign use

Items can persist between missions, but should have caps to avoid runaway snowballing.

Recommended approach:

- heroes keep relics;
- common items reset or enter armory;
- campaign choices unlock relic variants.
